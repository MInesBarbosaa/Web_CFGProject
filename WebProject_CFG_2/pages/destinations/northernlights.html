Northern Lights

My dream destination

The northern lights (Aurora borealis) aren't exactly a place on Earth. They are a naturally occurring atmospheric phenomenon that can happen in many places around the Northern hemisphere, between 60 and 75 degrees of latitude. They are a dancing frenzy between the goodess of dawn, Aurora, and Boreas, the northern wind. There' s a corresponding phenomenon in the South hemisphere, the Aurora australis. As I live between 37 and 42 degrees of latitude, Auroras are one of my dream destinations.

But what are Auroras after all? When a solar storm comes towards Earth some of the energy and small particles can travel down the magnetic field lines at the north and south poles into Earth's atmosphere. There, the particles interact with gases in our atmosphere resulting in beautiful displays of light in the sky - Auroras. Oxygen gives off green and red light.
